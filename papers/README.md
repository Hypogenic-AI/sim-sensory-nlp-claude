# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (Deep-read)

1. **wang2025_words_make_llms_perceive.pdf** — Wang, Isola, Cheung (2025). "Words That Make Language Models Perceive." Sensory prompting steers LLM representations toward vision/audio encoder alignment. Code: github.com/sophicle/sensory.

2. **hicke2025_zero_body_problem.pdf** — Hicke, Hamilton, Mimno (2025). "The Zero Body Problem." Compares sensory language use across 19 LLMs vs humans. Linear probes show models can identify sensory language; RLHF may discourage its use.

3. **marjieh2023_llms_predict_sensory_judgments.pdf** — Marjieh et al. (2023). "LLMs predict human sensory judgments across six modalities." GPT models recover perceptual structures (color wheel, pitch spiral) from text alone.

4. **hicke2025_zero_body_problem.pdf** — Hicke, Hamilton, Mimno (2025). "The Zero Body Problem." Large-scale LLM sensory language comparison.

## Supporting Papers (Abstract-read)

5. **xu2023_conceptual_representation_embodiment.pdf** — Xu et al. (2023). "Does Conceptual Representation Require Embodiment?" LLMs align with humans in non-sensorimotor but not sensorimotor domains.

6. **lee2024_lms_dont_learn_physical.pdf** — Lee & Lim (2024). "Language Models Don't Learn the Physical Manifestation of Language." H-Test benchmark showing LLMs struggle with physical properties.

7. **jones2024_embodied_simulation.pdf** — Jones & Trott (2024). "Multimodal Language Models Show Evidence of Embodied Simulation." MLLMs are sensitive to implicit visual features like object shape.

8. **abdou2021_perceptual_structure_color.pdf** — Abdou et al. (2021). "Can Language Models Encode Perceptual Structure Without Grounding?" Color embeddings in LLMs align with perceptual color space.

9. **bisk2020_experience_grounds_language.pdf** — Bisk et al. (2020). "Experience Grounds Language." Foundational paper on grounding as a spectrum.

10. **chalmers2024_thought_sensory_grounding.pdf** — Chalmers (2024). "Does Thought Require Sensory Grounding?" Philosophical analysis; argues pure thinkers are possible in principle.

11. **kennington2021_enriching_lm_sensorimotor.pdf** — Kennington (2021). "Enriching Language Models with Visually-grounded Word Vectors and Lancaster Sensorimotor Norms." Enriching ELECTRA/RoBERTa with sensorimotor data improves downstream tasks.

12. **derby2020_contextualised_semantic_features_bert.pdf** — Derby et al. (2020). "Deriving Contextualised Semantic Features from BERT." Extracts sensory features from BERT embeddings.

13. **lee2025_multimodal_perception_perceptual_strength.pdf** — Lee et al. (2025). "Exploring Multimodal Perception in LLMs Through Perceptual Strength Ratings." 21 models evaluated on Lancaster norms.

14. **wu2026_finetuning_sensorimotor.pdf** — Wu et al. (2026). "How does fine-tuning improve sensorimotor representations in LLMs?" RSA-based analysis of fine-tuning effects.

15. **siedenburg2023_language_sounds_unheard.pdf** — Siedenburg & Saitis (2023). "The language of sounds unheard." ChatGPT rates musical timbres partially matching humans.

16. **merullo2025_phonetic_representations_llama.pdf** — Merullo et al. (2025). "I Have No Mouth, and I Must Rhyme." Internal phonetic representations in LLaMA.

17. **yang2025_visual_grounding_embodied.pdf** — Yang et al. (2025). "Does Visual Grounding Enhance Embodied Knowledge?" VLMs don't outperform text-only on sensory tasks.

18. **gupta2026_words_that_make_sense.pdf** — Gupta et al. (2026). "Words that make SENSE." Sensorimotor norms from word embeddings.

19. **le2024_can_transformers_smell.pdf** — Le et al. (2024). "Can Transformers Smell Like Humans?" Probing olfactory representations.

20. **kang2024_sniff_ai.pdf** — Kang et al. (2024). "Sniff AI." Exploring LLM alignment with human smell experiences.
